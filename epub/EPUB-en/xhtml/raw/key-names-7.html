Basic facts (lesson 7)

Basic names

Vladimir Ilyich Lenin (Ulyanov) (1870-1924) –statesman and party leader, one of the founders and the leader of the Bolshevik party. Lenin was the organizer of the armed uprising in Petrograd in October 1917 (October Revolution). He was the Chairman of the Bolshevik government - the Council of People's Commissars (Sovnarkom) (1917-1924).

Joseph Vissarionovich Stalin (Dzhugashvili) (1879-1953) –statesman and party leader. He was General Secretary of the Central Committee of the All-Union Communist Party of the Bolsheviks (from 1922). During the Great Patriotic War, Stalin was the Chairman of the State Defense Committee, people's commissar of defense (until March 1947), Chairman of the Council of People’s Commissars (Sovnarkom). He was Marshal of the Soviet Union (1943), Generalissimo of the Soviet Union (1945).

Konstantin Eduardovich Tsiolkovsky (1857-1935) –Russian scientist and inventor, founder of modern cosmonautics.

Mikhail Alexandrovich Sholokhov (1905-1984) –Soviet writer and public figure. The author of the book “Tales of the Don” (1926), the novels "And Quiet Flows the Don", "Virgin Soil Upturned", "They Fought for Their Country",the story "Fate of a Man" and others. Sholokhov was the Nobel Prize winner in literature (1965), Twice Hero of Socialist Labor.

Dmitry Dmitriyevich Shostakovich (1906-1975) – Soviet composer, pianist, teacher. He was People's Artist of the USSR. Shostakovich is the author of a number of symphonies, operas (“The Nose”, “Lady Macbeth of the Mtsensk District”), ballets (“The Golden Age”, “The Bright Stream”, etc.).

Aram Ilyich Khachaturian (1903-1978) –Soviet Armenian composer, conductor, teacher. He was People's Artist of the USSR, Hero of Socialist Labor. He is the author of the music of the National Anthem of the Armenian SSR. He composed the music of the ballet “Gayane”, “Spartacus” and symphonic works.
