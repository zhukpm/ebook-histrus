Basic facts (lesson 1)

Basic concepts

Boyars (from Turkic bajar - a warrior, a rich man) - originally senior warriors, with whom the prince discussed issues of management and protection of his land. Gradually they turned into landowners.

Veche - people’s assembly of the East Slavic male warriors, at which the most important issues of internal life were discussed.

Druzhina - union of armed people, who united around the prince, participated in wars and in the management of territories under the rule of the prince.

The Golden Horde - state formed in 1236-1242 by Batu Khan during the Mongol conquests. It covered the territory of Eastern Europe, Kazakhstan, Western Siberia. It included the lands of North-Eastern Russia.

Prince - leader of a tribe, head of state. He was an elected ruler of the East Slavs, then a hereditary ruler; he performed military, judicial and other functions.

Chronicles - monuments of historical writing and literature of Ancient Rus. They were narrated on an annual basis in chronological order (the story of the events of each year began with the words "In the summer ...").

Monarchy - state whose head is a monarch (emperor, tsar, king, prince, etc.)

Community - association of people, which is characterized by common ownership of the means of production, self-government, community consciousness and traditions.

Tribe - union of several clans that had common territory, economy, cults and customs, common self-identity and self-designation.

Fragmentation - weakening of the power of the Kiev prince and the disintegration of the united Rus into separate principalities ("lands").

“Russkaya Pravda” (Russian Justice) - the code of laws of Ancient Rus.

Intestine war - discord, internal struggle.

Kholops - dependent people (slaves) of Ancient Rus.

Christianity - one of the three world religions (along with Buddhism and Islam). It has three main branches: Catholicism (Western Christianity), Orthodoxy (Eastern Christianity), Protestantism. The common feature that unites all the branches of Christianity is faith in Jesus Christ as God-man, the world's savior. The main source of creed is Scripture (Bible).

Paganism - complex of pre-Christian ideas. Paganism idolized the forces of nature and was characterized by polytheism. In contrast to paganism, Christianity placed the all-encompassing personality of God over nature (hence, monotheism).

Label (from Turkic jarlyq - command, order) - letters of the Golden Horde khans. They gave the right to the Russian princes to control a certain territory.
