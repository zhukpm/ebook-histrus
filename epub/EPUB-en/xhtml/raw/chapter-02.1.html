Formation of the Russian unified state

Formation of the Russian unified state

At the beginning of the XIV century, the fragmentation of the Russian land continued. In the north-east, there was struggle between Moscow and Tver for the right to get a label from the Golden Horde and to collect tribute.

In the first half of the XIV century, Moscow princes entered into an alliance with the Orthodox Church. This helped strengthen the position of Moscow.

Grand Duke of Moscow Dmitry Ivanovich (1359-1389) subjugated a number of Russian principalities and began the fight against the Horde. In 1380 , in the Battle of Kulikovo , the Russian soldiers, united by patriotism, common faith and unified leadership, for the first time inflicted a major defeat on the forces of the Golden Horde of Khan Mamay on the river Don. Moscow finally secured the role of a unifier, and its prince began to be called Dmitry Donskoy .

Ivan III

At the end of the XV century, under Ivan III the Great (1462-1505), the gathering of the lands of northeastern Russia under the guidance of Moscow was completed. There began the history of the Tsardom of Russia . Only Novgorod wanted to maintain independence and its republican system.

Then Ivan III organized a campaign, and in 1471he defeated the Novgorod militia. In 1478, Novgorod became part of the Moscow State. In 1485, there was abolished independence of Tver Principality.

As a result of the wars with Lithuania (the late XV–early XVI century), its border lands also became part of the Moscow state. Under Vasily III (1505-1533), the son of Ivan III, Pskov (1510), Smolensk (1514) and Ryazan (1521) were annexed. Thus, Gathering of the Lands of the unified state was completed.

In 1480 , Golden Horde khan Akhmat decided to restore his domination over Russia andto force Moscow to pay tribute again. To this end, he approached the borders of Rus. The troops of Ivan III closed the road to the Tatars and stood on the banks of the river Ugra (about two hundred kilometers southwest of Moscow). The khan’s attempts to cross the river were repelled by the Russian troops. Thus, the 240-year dependence of Russia on the Golden Horde ended.

The formation of the unified state helped create a well-armed and trained troop, and this was the reason for the victory of Moscow.

Russian boyars of the XVI-XVII centuries

In the Moscow state, there was strengthened the power of the Grand Duke, who got the title of the sovereign . However, his autocracy was limited by the traditional governing bodies and the rule of law. There was preserved the deliberative Boyar Duma, where the boyars could express their opinions.

The main military force of the unified state was the army, which was composed of servicemen. For the performance of their military duties, they gota land plot - estate .

In 1497 , there was adopted the Law Code (Sudebnik of 1497) ,the collection of laws of the unified state. It determined the norms of punishment for various crimes. For example, murderers and traitors were sentenced to death. The Law Code introduced a general rule for the entire state, according to which once a year, on November 26 (St. George's Day – the church holiday dedicated to St. George the Victorious), a peasant was allowed to leave his master (feudal lord).
