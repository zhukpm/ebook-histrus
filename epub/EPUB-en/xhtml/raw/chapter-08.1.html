Initial period of the Great Patriotic War

In 1933, Adolf Hitler came to power in Germany. He was preparing for the war. Due to the disagreement between the USSR and Western countries, it was not possible to maintain peace and to stop fascist Germany. On September 1, 1939 , Germany attacked Poland. The Second World War began. It was the result of the conflict of two coalitions: on the one hand, Germany, Italy, Finland, Hungary, Romania and others, on the other hand, Great Britain, France, and the USA. Hitler occupied the whole of Europe. He strove for world domination.

On June 22, 1941 , the army of Nazi Germany attacked the USSR. Hitler set a goal to wage a quick war, to capture Moscow and destroy the USSR before the winter, and to enslave its peoples.

For the Soviet people, the Great Patriotic War began.

The fact that it was the liberation war contributed to the Soviet people’s national conscious-raising and patriotic uplift. The whole country stood up to defend their Motherland. The peoples of all the USSR republics fought in the Red Army.

For the operational command of the hostilities, at the very beginning of the war there was created the State Defense Committee headed by Stalin.

The just struggle of the Soviet people was supported by Great Britain and the USA. Thus, the anti-Hitler coalition began to be formed. The United States sent arms, equipment, and foodstuffs to the USSR.

The first months of the war were unsuccessful for the Red Army, largely due to the fault of the Soviet leadership and Stalin personally. The German army occupied the Baltic republics, Byelorussia, part of Ukraine, and Moldavia. In September 1941, the siege of Leningrad began.

In autumn 1941, the German troops approached Moscow. The Battle of Moscow began. On December 5, 1941, the Soviet troops under the command of G.K. Zhukov began a counteroffensive near Moscow. By the beginning of 1942, the German army had been driven back 100-250 kilometers from the capital.
