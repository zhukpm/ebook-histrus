Economic and socio-political development of the USSR in 1945-1985

After the end of the Second World War, the authority of the USSR in the world rose. At the same time, a conflict occurred between the two most powerful countries - the USSR and the USA, which had nuclear weapons. Thus, the Cold War began.

After the war, the main task for the USSR was the restoration of the economy. 6,200 plants and factories were built and restored. There was largely developed heavy industry. The military industry was strengthened. In the difficult conditions, collective farmers provided the country with food.

In the post-war period, the country was in a difficult political situation. The party leadership controlled the life of the people. The personality cult of Stalin was maintained.

After the death of J. Stalin, N.S. Khrushchev (1953-1964) became the leader of the party and state. He criticized the personality cult of Stalin and eliminated the repression system. It contributed to the social upsurge in the country and development of culture.

There occurred important changes in the economy of the USSR. Above all, some tasks in the sphere of agriculture were solved: taxes on peasant farming were reduced, and purchase prices for agricultural products were increased. There began the development of new lands (virgin soils) in Siberia and Kazakhstan. The salary of workers and employees increased. Housing construction was actively developing. More than 50 million people received new apartments.

However, Khrushchev made many economic and political mistakes. Thus, in 1954, on his initiative and without a referendum, the Supreme Council of the USSR adopted the decree on the transfer of the Crimean peninsula (then it was the Crimean region) from the RSFSR to the Ukrainian SSR. This decision laid the future contradictions in the relationship between Russia and Ukraine.

After Khrushchev’s resignation, it was L. I. Brezhnev (1964-1982) who became the new leader of the country. During those years, in the economy of the USSR there were actively developing oil, gas and military industries. The population got free housing, education, and medical treatment. However, in the 1970s the situation in the industry and agriculture worsened. New reforms were needed.

In foreign policy , the USSR achieved equality with the United States in terms of missile and nuclear weapons. There were successfully developing Soviet-American economic and political relations. However, the entry of the Soviet troops on the territory of Afghanistan in 1979 worsened the relations between the USSR and Western countries.
