East Slavs and the formation of the Old Russian state

From ancient times, the tribes of Slavs lived in the territory of Central and Eastern Europe. In the VII-VIII centuries, the East Slavs, the ancestors of the modern Russian, Ukrainian, and Belarusian peoples, separated from a single community. They explored the vast forest areas of Eastern Europe, where Finnish and Baltic tribes lived. In the east, their neighbors were Turkic peoples (Volga Bulgars, Polovtsy, etc.). The East Slavs were farmers , but they were also involved in cattle breeding , hunting and fishing . In the IX-X centuries, there appeared the Slavs’ towns. They were mainly located along the banks of rivers, and they gradually became centers of trade and craft (the manufacture of iron tools, pottery, fabrics, etc.). The largest city was Kiev. It stood on the Dnieper River, on which the main trade route passed. Due to its location, Kiev became a major economic and political center of the East Slavs. From the end of the IX century, princes from the Rurik dynasty came there with their druzhinas (professional military).

Kiev became the capital of the Old Russian state. In the X century, the power of the Kiev princes spread to the territories inhabited by the East Slavs and other neighboring tribes. All of them paid tribute (tax) to the Kiev prince, who protected them from enemies. Under the leadership of the princes, military campaigns were waged against rich Byzantium (it was the name of the Eastern Roman Empire, which had a capital - the city of Byzantium (Constantinople) – now Istanbul). The successful campaigns strengthened the financial position and power of the prince with his druzhina and contributed to the development of foreign trade. At the same time, Ancient Rus was formed on the East Slavic basis with the participation of other peoples (Normans, Khazars, etc.), i.e. it was originally a multinational state.

The Old Russian state was finally formed at the end of the X century, during the reign of St. Vladimir (980-1015). In 988 , Vladimir introduced Christianity in its Greek Orthodox (Byzantine) form, as he hoped that it would strengthen his power, as well as the ties of Rus with Byzantium.

The Old Russian state protected the land from the attacks of nomads and provided favorable conditions for the development of European states. Thus, Rus became a kind of bridge that connected the West and the East.
