Basic facts (lesson 4)

Basic names

Peter I (the Great) (1672-1725) - Russian Tsar from 1682 (he reigned from 1689 - jointly with Ivan V, from 1696 - autocratically). He was the first Russian emperor (from 1721).

Catherine II (1729-1796) -Russian Empress (1762-1796). She pursued the policy of enlightened absolutism.

Mikhail Vasilyevich Lomonosov (1711-1765) - the first Russian scientist. He was a world-class naturalist, a man of encyclopedic knowledge, diverse interests and abilities, one of the founders of physical chemistry, a poet. He laid the foundations of the modern Russian literary language. Lomonosov was a historian; he advocated the development of independent Russian science.

Alexander Vasilyevich Suvorov (1730-1800) - commander and military theorist. For his outstanding military success, he obtained the highest military rank of generalissimo. For his willful character he was twice sent to exile. He is the author of the work “Science of Victory”.
