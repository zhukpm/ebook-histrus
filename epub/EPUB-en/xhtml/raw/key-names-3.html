Basic facts (lesson 3)

Basic names

Boris Fyodorovich Godunov (circa 1552-1605) - RussianTsarfrom 1598 (elected by the ZemskySobor).

False Dmitry I(1580-1606) - impostor, who impersonated Tsarevich Dmitry; he was the tsar in 1605-1606. According to the official documents of Boris Godunov’s government, his real name was Grigory Otrepyev. In 1605, he entered Moscow. In 1606, he was assassinated.

Kuzma Minin (Sukhoruk) (date of birth unknown - 1616) - one of the organizers and leaders of the Second Zemsky Militia, Nizhny Novgorod artisan. In 1613, for his services he joined the Boyar Duma.

Dmitry Mikhailovich Pozharsky (1578-1642) –statesman and military leader, prince, boyar (from 1613), Kuzma Minin’s associate. He was one of the leaders of the Second Militia that liberated Moscow from the Polish troops (1612).

Mikhail Fyodorovich Romanov (1596-1645) - the first Russian Tsar from the Romanov dynasty. He was the son of boyar Fyodor Nikitich Romanov (Patriarch Filaret). In 1613, the Zemsky Sobor elected him to the Russian throne.

Nikon (1605-1681) - patriarch, church leader and statesman. In 1652, he became Patriarch. In1653, he began to implement the church reform. In 1658, he came into conflict with the Tsar and arbitrarily left Moscow. The Zemsky Sobor of 1666-1667 approved Nikon's reforms, but deprived him of the sacerdotal functions.

Stepan Timofeyevich Razin (circa 1630-1671)-leader of the uprising of Cossacks and peasants in 1667-1671.He was the Don Cossack.
