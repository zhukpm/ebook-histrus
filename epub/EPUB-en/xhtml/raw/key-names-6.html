Basic facts (lesson 6)

Basic names

Nicholas II (1868-1918) –Russian emperor (1894-1917). He abdicated during the Russian Revolution of 1917.

Vladimir Ilyich Lenin (Ulyanov) (1870-1924) –statesman and party leader. He was one of the founders of the RSDLP and the head of the RSDLP (Bolsheviks) from 1903, as well as the ideologist and leader of the October Uprising of 1917 (October Revolution). In 1917-1924, he was the Chairman of the Soviet Government –the Council of People's Commissars (Sovnarkom).

Anton Pavlovich Chekhov (1860-1904) - Russian writer, prominent classic of world literature. He was a doctor by profession. Chekhov is one of the most famous playwrights in the world. His works have been translated into more than 100 languages. His plays, especially “The Seagull”, “The Three Sisters” and “The Cherry Orchard”, have been staged for more than 100 years in many theaters of the world.
