Basic facts (lesson 8)

Questions and answers

  1. What were the goals of Hitler and his allies in the war against the USSR?
    Hitler strove for world domination. He set a goal to wage a quick war, to conquer Moscow and destroy the USSR before the winter, and to enslave its peoples. He pursued the policy of slaughter of civilians in the occupied Soviet territories, primarily Slavs and Jews.
  2. What measures were taken by the Soviet leadership and society to strengthen the country's defense during the war?
    To run the military operations, on June 30, 1941, there was created the State Defense Committee, which was headed by Joseph Stalin. The slogan of the wartime economy was “Everything for the front, everything for the victory!” The main food bases during the war were the Volga region, Siberia, Kazakhstan, Central Asia. The Russian Orthodox Church and representatives of other religions strengthened the spirit of the Red Army and provided it with material assistance.
  3. Describe the initial period of the Great Patriotic War.
    The first months of the war were unsuccessful for the Red Army due to the fault of the Soviet leadership and Stalin personally. The Germans occupied Latvia, Lithuania, Byelorussia, Right-bank Ukraine, Moldavia. In September, the siege of Leningrad began. The German army approached Moscow.
  4. What countries were the allies of the USSR in the struggle against fascist Germany?
    The allies of the USSR in the struggle against fascist Germany were Great Britain and the USA.
  5. Speak about the Battle of Moscow.
    In the autumn of 1941, the German troops approached Moscow. On September 30,there began the Battle of Moscow. On December 5, 1941, the Red Army, under the command of G.K. Zhukov, launched a counterattack near Moscow. By the beginning of 1942, the Germans had retreated 100-250 kilometers from the capital. It was the first important victory of the Soviet troops in the Great Patriotic War.
  6. Due to what was the radical change during the Great Patriotic War?
    In the period from autumn 1942 to the end of 1943, there occurred the radical change in the war. It was due to the victory of the Red Army in the Battle of Stalingrad. On February 2, 1943, the German army was encircled near Stalingrad. A large group of German troops was captured, including 24 generals. It was an important victory, which showed the advantage of the Soviet troops. As a result of the general offensive of the Red Army, the siege of Leningrad was broken in January 1943. By summer, a significant part of the North Caucasus had been liberated.
  7. What do you know about the policy of Nazi Germany in the occupied territories?
    In the territory occupied by the German army, Nazi Germany resorted to terror and the policy of slaughter of the Slavic and Jewish population. Concentration camps were created. In total, about seven million civilians and about four million prisoners of war died in the occupied territories. More than five million people were taken away for work to Germany.
  8. What was the significance of the victory of the USSR in the struggle against Nazi Germany?

    During the Great Patriotic War, the Soviet people not only defended their national independence, but also saved the world from fascism. More than 27 million Soviet citizens died in that war. The authority of the USSR in the world became very high.

    After the war, a new system of international relations was formed. New state borders were established in Eastern Europe. In order to maintain peace and develop contacts between countries, there was created the United Nations (UN). The USSR became a member of the UN.
