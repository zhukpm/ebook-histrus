Basic facts (lesson 5)

Basic names

Alexander I (1801-1825) –Russian emperor. In the early years of the reign, he made some liberal transformations.

Nicholas I (1825-1855) –Russian emperor. The Crimean War that began during his reign ended in defeat.

Alexander II (1855-1881) –Russian emperor. The period of the reign of Alexander II was called the era of great reforms, since serfdom was abolished (therefore, in official literature Alexander II was called Tsar Liberator); the judicial and zemstvo systems were reformed, and the army was rebuilt.

Mikhail Illarionovich Kutuzov (1745-1813) - outstanding military commander, diplomat, statesman, Field Marshall General (1812), His Serene Highness, Prince (1812).He took part in the Russian-Turkish wars of 1768-1774 and 1787-1791. In August 1812, he was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Russian army.

Sergey Ivanovich Muravyov-Apostol (1796-1826) - lieutenant colonel, Decembrist, one of the leaders of the uprising of the Chernigov regiment. He was sentencedtodeath.

Pavel Ivanovich Pestel (1793-1826) - Decembrist, Colonel. He was the organizer of the “Southern Society” and the author of its political program (“Russkaya Pravda”). He was sentenced to death.

Kondraty Fyodorovich Ryleyev (1795-1826) - Decembrist, poet. On December 14, 1825,he virtually led the uprising in St. Petersburg, ordering about in the Senate Square. He was sentenced to death.

Dmitry Ivanovich Mendeleev (1834-1907) –Russian scientist and chemist of the late XIX century. Among his most famous discoveries is the periodic law of chemical elements;it is one of the fundamental laws of the universe.

Alexander Stepanovich Popov (1859-1905) –Russian physicist and electrical engineer, professor, inventor of radio (1895).

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky (1821-1881) - great Russian writer, thinker of the second half of the XIX century. He is the author of the novels “Crime and Punishment”, “The Brothers Karamazov” and others.

Lev Nikolayevich Tolstoy (1828-1910) - great Russian writer, philosopher, religious thinker of the late XIX - early XX century. He is the author of the novels “War and Peace”, “Anna Karenina”, etc.

Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky (1840-1893) - great Russian composer, conductor of the second half of the XIX century. He is the author of the world famous ballets “Swan Lake”, “Sleeping Beauty”, “The Nutcracker”; he also composed more than 100 romances.
