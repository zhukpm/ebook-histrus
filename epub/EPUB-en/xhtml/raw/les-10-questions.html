Basic facts (lesson 10)

Questions and answers

  1. What is the national composition of modern Russia?
    Russia is a multinational country. People of 160 nationalities live here.
  2. What are the main religions in Russia?
    Russia is a multi-religious country. Most people profess Christianity (mainly Orthodoxy). Other traditional religions are Islam, Judaism, and Buddhism.
  3. What economic reforms were carried out in Russia in the 1990s?
    In 1991, in the country there began economic reforms. The main goal of the reforms was to establish a market economy in Russia. There were introduced free prices in the country. The privatization process began. Defense spending fell. There appeared many goods. At the same time, the living standards of most people worsened.
  4. What new class appeared in Russia as a result of the economic reforms?
    As a result of the economic reforms, there was a division into the rich and the poor; the living standards of the greater part of the population declined. There appeared a new class â€“ the middle class that supported market reforms. In addition, there was formed a layer of large private owners (bankers, entrepreneurs).
  5. What impact did the economic reforms have on the material situation of the population of Russia?
    As a result of the reforms, there appeared new opportunities for people. Some of them got good jobs at state and private enterprises. However, a significant part of the Russian population, primarily retired people, began to live worse. In general, the living standards of the Russian population declined.
  6. What changes occurred in the state system of Russia after the adoption of the 1993 Constitution?

    On December 12, 1993, the Constitution of Russia was adopted. Under the Constitution, Russia is a democratic federal state with the republican form of government. The Federal Assembly(parliament) became the legislative body of the Russian Federation. It consists of two houses: the Upper House - the Federation Council, the Lower House - State Duma (the elected body).

    The head of the executive power is the President of the Russian Federation. In Russia, there also appeared the new state symbols: the coat of arms, the flag and the anthem of the Russian Federation.

  7. In what way were the relations between Russia and Western countries developing in the 1990s?

    Russia pursued a new foreign policy. It had lost its allies in Eastern and Central Europe. The Cold War ended. There were developing the relations with the USA. Along with the United States and Western countries, Russia fought against terrorism and drug trafficking. There began the process of Russia's integration into the world market.

    In 1996, Russia was admitted to the Council of Europe. The economic cooperation of the Russian Federation with foreign countries expanded. Russia became a member of the G7. This organization began to be called the G8 of the leading economic powers. Russia also became a member of the International Monetary Fund (IMF).
