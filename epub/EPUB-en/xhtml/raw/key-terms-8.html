Basic concepts

The anti-Hitler coalition – alliance of states and peoples that fought in the Second World War against Nazi Germany and its allies. The USSR was the decisive force. Based on the coalition, there was established the United Nations (UN).

The Second Front – front of the armed struggle of the USA and Great Britain as well as the troops of a number of allied states against Nazi Germany in 1944-1945. It was opened in Western Europe on June 6, 1944: Anglo-American troops landed in Northern France (in Normandy).

Occupation - seizure by the armed forces (army and navy) of a state, a territory that does not belong to it. It is usually temporary.

The United Nations (UN) – the international organization that was created on June 26, 1945. The organization’s goal is to strengthen international peace and security and develop cooperation between states. The UN Charter was signed by representatives of 50 states. The USSR became a member of the UN Security Council.
