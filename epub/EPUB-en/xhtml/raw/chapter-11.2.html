Political development of modern Russia

At present, civil society is being formed in Russia: there is a reform of the constitutional arrangement, political and legal systems, public authorities and local self-government.

An important role in the development of civil society is played by the public organizations - the Presidential Council for Civil Society and Human Rights, the Public Chamber of the Russian Federation, and the institution of the Commissioner for Human Rights in the Russian Federation. Their goal is to protect the rights and freedoms of citizens of the Russian Federation, public associations and foreign nationals in the Russian Federation.

In 2000, in Russia there were created seven federal districts headed by plenipotentiaries of the president of the Russian Federation (Central , Ural , Volga (Privolzhsky), Far Eastern, Northwestern, Siberian, Southern).

In 2001,there was created the State Council of the Russian Federation (the State Council) - an advisory body under the President of the Russian Federation. The State Council consists of the leaders of the constituent entities of the Federation and considers vital issues.

Nowadays, there are more than 100 political parties in the country. The main parties of modern Russia, which have their own factions in the State Duma, are the United Russia party, the Communist Party of the Russian Federation (CPRF), the Liberal Democratic Party of Russia (LDPR), A Just Russia party.
