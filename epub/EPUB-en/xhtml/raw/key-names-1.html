Basic facts (lesson 1)

Basic names

Rurik (date of birth unknown - 879?) – leader of the Varangian (Scandinavian) squad. In 862,the East Slavs called him to reign in Novgorod. He is considered the founder of the Rurik dynasty.

Vladimir I (Svyatoslavovich) (date of birth unknown - 1015) -Grand Prince of Kiev (from 980). In 988,he proclaimed Christianity as the state religion (Saint Vladimir). He completed the creation of the Old Russian state.

Yaroslav the Wise (circa 978-1054) -Grand Duke of Kiev (from 1019).

Yuri Dolgoruky (1090s - 1157) -Prince of Suzdal, son of Vladimir Monomakh. In his father’s lifetime, he ruled the Rostov-Suzdal land. After the death of Grand Duke of Kiev, Mstislav the Great, he began to rule independently and conducted active struggle to becomePrince of Kiev (hence the nickname Dolgoruky). He is considered the founder of Moscow.

Andrei Bogolyubsky (circa 1111-1174) - Yuri Dolgoruky’s son. On his initiative, there were built the Assumption Cathedral in Vladimir and the Church of the Intercession on the Nerl.

Alexander Nevsky (1220-1263) -Prince of Novgorod, Grand Prince of Vladimir (from 1252). His name is associated with the victories over the Swedes (the Battle of the Neva, 1240) and over German knights (the Battle on the Ice, 1242). He rejected the offer of the Pope to adopt Catholicism and enter into an alliance against the Mongols. Thanks to his policy, the western borders of Rus were protected, and dependence on the Golden Horde was somewhat weakened.
