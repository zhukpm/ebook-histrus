Basic facts (lesson 1)

Questions and answers

  1. Who were the East Slavs? Where did they live and what did they do?
    The East Slavs were the tribes of the Slavs who from ancient times inhabited Central and Eastern Europe. They were engaged in agriculture and cattle breeding.
  2. When and how was the Old Russian state formed?
    The city of Kiev, the largest city of Ancient Rus, became the economic and political center of the East Slavs. The East Slavs and their neighbors paid tribute to the Prince of Kiev, and when in 988 Prince Vladimir introduced a single religion, Christianity, the Old Russian state was finally formed.
  3. When and under what prince did Christianization of Rus take place? What role did Christianity play in the life of Ancient Rus?
    Prince Vladimir Christianized Rus in 988, and thus, based on a single state religion, the formation of the Old Russian state was completed. Christianity influenced the development of the culture and life of the Russian people.
  4. What period is the heyday of Ancient Rus?
    Ancient Rus reached its heyday under Yaroslav the Wise (1019-1054), the son of Prince Vladimir. Under Yaroslav, the international authority of Rus grew and culture developed.
  5. What was the fragmentation of the Russian lands? What were its consequences? What were the largest principalities of the era of fragmentation?
    After the death of Prince Yaroslav the Wise and the division of his lands, there began the period of political fragmentation. By the end of the XII century, instead of a single Old Russian state, 15 principalities had appeared (the Principality of Kiev, Vladimir-Suzdal principality, the Principality of Smolensk, etc.) with the common faith and language. There were intestine wars among the princes. The political fragmentation weakened Rus.
  6. What are the main monuments of Old Russian culture?
    The main monuments of Old Russian culture were the Cathedral of St. Sophia in Kiev and the Cathedral of St. Sophia in Novgorod, the Assumption Cathedral in Vladimir, Vladimir Icon of the Mother of God; the main literary monuments were chronicles.
  7. What were the reasons for the defeat of the Russian principalities in the fight against the Mongols in the Ð¥III century?
    The Russian land was not united. It was the period of political fragmentation and constant intestine wars among the princes. The Mongols turned out to be stronger militarily.
  8. How did the Golden Horde exercise power over Rus?
    Rus became part of the Golden Horde, but it retained its internal practices; its economic dependence was limited to paying tribute. The political dependence was that the Russian princes gained from the Golden Horde khan the right to reign.
