Culture of Ancient Rus (X-XIII centuries)

The nature of the development of Russian culture was determined by Orthodoxy, which came from Byzantium. Religion influenced the spread of writing (the very appearance of writing in Rus was associated with the activities of the brothers Cyril and Methodius at the end of the IX century. They created the Slavic alphabet - the Cyrillic alphabet, based on which the modern Russian alphabet arose).

In the XI century, there appeared translations of church and secular books. At monasteries, schools were opened. Literacy was widespread among the urban population.

The most important phenomenon of Rus culture was chronicles . The author of the most famous one - "The Tale of Bygone Years" (1113) was monk Nestor . Russian chronicles were written in the native language, while in Western Europe, books were written in Latin - the language that was foreign to the people.

The most famous literary monument of Ancient Rus is the "Tale of Igor's Campaign" (the end of the XII century). Its author opposed the intestine wars that weakened Rus in the fight against external enemies, and called for the unity of the Russian lands.

In ancient Rus, architecture and art were developing.

In the XI century, the largest temples were built: the Cathedral of St. Sophia in Kiev and the Cathedral of St. Sophia in Novgorod .

The most significant building of North-Eastern Rus was the Assumption Cathedral in Vladimir. It was icons that were the necessary element of the temples. The most revered icon in Rus was the image of the Mother of God with a baby in her arms (Vladimir Icon of the Mother of God ).

Крест символизирует распятие Иисуса Христа

The religious symbol of Christianity is the cross (the cross symbolizes the crucifixion of Jesus Christ).

The new religion became the spiritual foundation of the Russian people. The Orthodox Church not only taught, but also educated Old Russian society and fought against pagan remnants. The adoption of Orthodoxy by Rus influenced many features of its historical development.
