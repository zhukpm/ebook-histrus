Soviet science and culture

After the war, science and culture were developing, international contacts were expanding. In the field of atomic physics, such scientists as I.V. Kurchatov , A.D. Sakharov , L.D. Landau and others conducted active researches. In 1954, the world's first nuclear power plant was built in the USSR.

It was the exploration of space that was an important scientific and technological achievement of that time. Great success in space exploration was achieved by Soviet spacecraft designer S.P. Korolev. In 1957 , under his leadership there was created the world's first artificial Earth satellite.

On April 12, 1961 Soviet pilot Yu. A. Gagarin carried out the world's first manned flight into space. V.V. Tereshkova became the first female cosmonaut.

The first spacewalk of man was also first made by a Soviet cosmonaut –it was A. A. Leonov .

A new impetus was given to the creative work of the artistic intelligentsia; there were developing literature, art, theater. A characteristic feature of that time was the increased interest in poetry, including the poetry in national republics. Through his poetry and prose (“Songs of the mountains”, “My Dagestan”, “Take care of friends”, etc.),national poet of Dagestan Rasul Gamzatov conveyed to the reader the wisdom of his people, its moral experience, and the values of its artistic culture.

A new generation of poets appeared - A. Voznesensky , E. Yevtushenko , R. Rozhdestvensky and others.

In literature there was developing a new theme, whereas the old theme was deepening. In that period, the Nobel Prize winners in literature were Soviet writers B.L. Pasternak , M.A. Sholokhov , A.I. Solzhenitsyn , poet I.A. Brodsky . The theme of the Great Patriotic War gained another perspective. For the first time in Soviet literature there were touched upon political repressions - primarily in the works of A.I. Solzhenitsyn ("The Gulag Archipelago" and others).

In the works of Kyrgyz writer Ch.T. Aitmatov (“The White Ship”, “Spotted Dog Running on Seashore”, etc.), which are associated with folk myths and national folklore, there were revealed the depths of folk history, its moral, labor and humanistic traditions.

Soviet theater and cinema experienced the new stage of their development. Soviet theater was becoming more open to the perception of various problems of modernity. A significant place in the cinema was occupied by the theme of the Great Patriotic War (the films “The Cranes Are Flying,” “Ballad of a Soldier,” “Fate of a Man”).

Famous Soviet film directors S. Gerasimov , E. Ryazanov , N. Mikhalkov contributed greatly to the development of cinema. Film director L.I. Gaidai made great comedies ("Kidnapping, Caucasian Style", "The Diamond Arm"). Soviet ballet was well-known all over the world.

Important changes occurred in architecture . After the war, there began the construction of multi-story buildings. Seven high-rise buildings, the so-called Stalin’s high-risers (Seven Sisters) , were constructed in Moscow - for example, the main building of Moscow State University on the Lenin Hills, the building of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the USSR in Smolenskaya Square, etc. They became the symbol of Moscow of the Soviet era.

In Moscow there were also built the Kremlin Palace of Congresses and the Ostankino Television Tower (its height is 553 meters).

During that period, particular attention was paid to the memory of the Great Patriotic War. In honor of the Great Victory, there were created a memorial complex on Mamaev Kurgan in Volgograd and a sculptural ensemble devoted to the defenders of the Brest Fortress in Byelorussia. In Moscow, near the Kremlin wall, there was opened the architectural ensemble "The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier" . Eternal Flame always burns at the grave.

At that time, there was a process of the expansion of international cultural contacts. In 1957, Moscow hosted the World Festival of Youth and Students, in which representatives from all continents participated. In 1960, in order to assist the countries of Asia, Africa, and Latin America in training national staff, in Moscow there was founded the University of Peoples' Friendship (currently the Peoples' Friendship University of Russia (RUDN University), in which representatives from over 140 countries study. In 1980, the XXII Summer Olympic Games were held in Moscow, which were the first Olympic Games held in a socialist country.

In general, the cultural transformations in the life of Soviet society were significant and represented one of the most important periods in the history of the USSR.
