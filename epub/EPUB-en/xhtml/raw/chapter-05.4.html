Culture of Russia in the XIX century

At the beginning of the XIX century, there was the heyday of Russian culture. New universities were opened (in Kazan, Kiev, St. Petersburg, etc.). The development of science was facilitated by the organization of the first Russian overseas expedition under the command of I.F. Krusenstern and Yu. F. Lisiansky (1803-1806). The expedition of F.F. Bellingshausen and M.P. Lazarev (1819-1821) discovered Antarctica.

First Russian circumnavigation route © Kaidor, Wikimedia Commons, CC BY‑SA 4.0

Science and technology achieved significant success. At the end of the XIX century D.I. Mendeleev created the periodic system of chemical elements.

In 1895, A.S. Popov demonstrated the world's first radio receiver.

I.P. Pavlov (the first Russian scientist - Noble-prize winner) created the doctrine of higher nervous activity.

It was literature that occupied a special position in culture. In the second quarter of the XIX century, there began to develop Russian realism . Its formation and heyday are associated with the work of A.S. Griboyedov (“Woe from Wit”), M. Yu. Lermontov (“Death of the poet”, “A hero of our time”), N.V. Gogol ("Dead Souls", "The Government Inspector").

A special place in Russian literature is occupied by great poet A.S. Pushkin , who is the creator of the modern Russian literary language and the author of such works as “Eugene Onegin”, “Ruslan and Lyudmila”, “Boris Godunov” and others.

In the era of liberal reforms in Russia, education was further developed. New universities and institutes were opened; there appeared female gymnasiums .

Several eras in the history of Russia were reflected in the works by L.N. Tolstoy (novels “War and Peace”, “Anna Karenina”, “Resurrection”).

In his novels (“Crime and Punishment”, “The Idiot”, “The Brothers Karamazov”, “Demons”), F.M. Dostoevsky posed religious and moral questions.

In the fine arts, there developed the creative work of the Peredvizhniki (I.N. Kramskoy , V.G. Perov , and others) who in their paintings depicted the everyday life of ordinary people; they held exhibitions in provincial towns. It was the landscapes of I.I. Shishkin and I.I. Levitan , historical paintings of I.E. Repin and V.I. Surikov that became well-known.

Sculptor M.O. Mikeshin created the composition "Millennium of Russia" in Novgorod.

The monument to A.S. Pushkin in Moscow by sculptor A.M. Opekushin is known to all Russian people.

The greatest composer of the era was P.I. Tchaikovsky , who composed operas (“Eugene Onegin”, “The Queen of Spades”) and great ballets (“Swan Lake”, “Sleeping Beauty”).

In the second half of the XIX century, the multinational Russian culture made a huge contribution to the development of world culture.
