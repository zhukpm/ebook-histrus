Basic facts (lesson 4)

Basic concepts

The Charter to the nobility of 1785 – it was granted by Catherine II. It affirmed the old privileges and introduced new privileges for the nobility.

The Charter to the towns of 1785 – it was granted by Catherine II. It led to the creation of new bodies of municipal self-government.

Colleges - central institutions of the XVIII - early ХIХ century. They managed individual branches of public administration.

Manufactory (from Lat. manus - hand and factura - production) – large industrial enterprise based on the division of labor and manual technique.

Entrepreneurship - economic activity aimed at systematic gaining of profits from the production and (or) sale of goods and provision of services.

Education - ideological direction, which was aimed at the elimination of ignorance, poverty, personal dependence.

The Senate - the highest state body in Russia which was subordinate to the emperor. It was established by Peter I in 1711. Later it was the highest judicial body.

The Synod - the state body for managing the affairs of the Russian Orthodox Church. It was established by PeterI in 1721.
