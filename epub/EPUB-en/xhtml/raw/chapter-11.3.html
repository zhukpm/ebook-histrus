Russia in the modern world

Russia's foreign policy is aimed at protecting its national interests. In the new environment, Russia and China oppose the global leadership of the USA in the world. Russia did not support the USA military operation in Iraq in 2003. In 2013, Russia won a diplomatic victory and prevented the USA from the military invasion of Syria.

The USA set a goal to include the former Soviet republics, primarily the Baltic States, Georgia and Ukraine, in the sphere of its influence. This brings the world back to the days of the Cold War and the struggle of the two systems.

At present, there are disagreements in Russian-American relations on Syrian issues, on the recognition of independence of South Ossetia, Abkhazia, and the situation in Ukraine.

V.V. Putin and President of the People's Republic of China Xi Jinping during a tour of the Moscow Kremlin © kremlin.ru

The eastern direction in the foreign policy of modern Russia also retains its significance. One of the main strategic partners of the Russian Federation is China. Russia is also developing the relations with India, Japan, Vietnam, and South Korea.

In recent years, there have been actively developing Russia's political, economic, and cultural ties with Latin American countries.

Since 2012 , Russia has been a member of the World Trade Organization (WTO).

The most important area in the foreign policy of the Russian Federation is the development of relations with the CIS countries. These relations are developing within the framework of the Union State of Russia and Belarus ; Eurasian Economic Community (EurAsEC) (its members are Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan).

Participants of the informal meeting of the CIS heads of state © kremlin.ru

In recent years, Russia has been pursuing a course towards strengthening Eurasian cooperation. On May 29, 2014 , President of the Russian Federation V.V. Putin, President of Belarus A.G. Lukashenko and President of Kazakhstan N.A. Nazarbayev signed an agreement on the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU) . The agreement guarantees the free movement of goods, services, capital and labor force within the borders of the three countries. Later, Armenia and Kyrgyzstan joined the treaty.

Within the CIS, there are difficulties and contradictions. Since the beginning of 2014, the political events in Ukraine have complicated Russian-Ukrainian relations. Under these conditions, the Russian state came out in defense of the Russian-speaking population of Crimea and the regions of Eastern Ukraine. On March 16, 2014, a referendum was held in Crimea. Almost 97% of the electors voted for the accession of Crimea to Russia (until 1954 Crimea had been its part). On March 18, 2014 in Moscow, the agreement was signed on the accession of the Republic of Crimea to the Russian Federation.

Despite the existing contradictions with the USA and Western countries, Russia is committed to developing a constructive dialogue with foreign partners in the field of international security and the fight against global terrorism. It is the United Nations that Russia considers as the main coordinating body in solving problems common to all humanity. Interaction within the framework of such organizations as the BRICS and the SCO remains an important area of modern foreign policy of the Russian Federation.
