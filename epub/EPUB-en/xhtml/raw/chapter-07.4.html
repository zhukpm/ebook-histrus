Soviet culture

In the USSR, education and culture were developing. After the revolution, there were many illiterate people in the USSR, especially in national areas. For example, in Tajikistan 99.5% of Tajiks were illiterate, in Uzbekistan 98.4% of Uzbeks could not read and write. Therefore, the country began work on the elimination of illiteracy.

Education in the USSR became universal, free for all nationalities. More than 40 nations of the Soviet Union first got their writing. Soviet culture was multinational .

Teaching the population to read and write in the village of Shorkasy, Cheboksary district, the 1930s

Soviet science was developing. Academicians S.V. Lebedev and I.M. Gubkin made a great contribution to the development of petrochemistry. Of great significance were the scientific researches of physicist A.F. Ioffe , biologists I.V. Michurin and N.I. Vavilov . K.E. Tsiolkovsky substantiated the use of rockets for flights into space. His main scientific works are related to rocket dynamics and cosmonautics.

In the 1920s, Soviet literature was actively developing. In that period, of great significance were the works of writers M.A. Bulgakov , M. Gorky , M.A. Sholokhov , poets V.V. Mayakovsky , S.A. Yesenin .

Soviet art was evolving. New monuments were created. V. Mukhina’s sculpture “Worker and Kolkhoz Woman” is a famous Soviet monument; it became a kind of emblem of the USSR (today one can see it in Moscow).

Architecture was developing. In Moscow and in other cities of the USSR, many beautiful monuments and buildings were constructed. In Moscow, in Red Square, a stone building of the Mausoleum was built (architect A. Shchusev ) - it is the monument-tomb of V.I. Lenin. In 1935, the first metro line in the USSR began to operate in the capital.

New theatrical art was developing. In the theaters there were staged the first Soviet plays written in the realistic tradition. In Soviet times, the best traditions of Russian ballet were preserved. Galina Ulanova was the most famous Soviet ballerina of that time. She ushered in an era of foreign touring of the Soviet ballet.

S.M. Eisenstein (the filmmaker of “Battleship Potemkin”) laid the foundation for the development of revolutionary themes in cinematography . In the 1930s, sound films were made. There appeared a new genre - musical comedy (the films “Volga-Volga” , “Circus” , “Jolly Fellows” ); historical films appeared - “Alexander Nevsky” , “Peter the Great” , “Ivan the Terrible” , etc.

Musical art was developing through the works of composers S.S. Prokofiev , A.I. Khachaturian , D.D. Shostakovich . Among the authors of mass songs and film music were V.I. Lebedev-Kumach , I.O. Dunayevsky .
