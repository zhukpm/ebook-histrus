Great October Socialist Revolution of 1917

Great October Socialist Revolution of 1917

After the beginning of World War I in 1914, Russia's economic situation became worse, and a political crisis broke out in the country. Workers, peasants and soldiers opposed the tsar and the government.

It became the cause of the Russian Revolution. It began in February 1917 with the revolutionary actions in Petrograd. After Petrograd, the revolution won in Moscow and further throughout the country.

On March 2, 1917, Russian emperor Nicholas II abdicated. Thus, the monarchy was abolished in the country.

New government bodies were created - the Soviet of Workers' and Soldiers' Deputies and the Provisional Government . Russia became one of the most democratic countries in Europe. Political rights and freedoms of citizens were proclaimed, death penalty was abolished, and national and religious restrictions were eliminated.

However, the government did not carry out economic reforms. In addition, Russia continued to participate in the First World War, although people protested in the capital and other cities. The fight against the Provisional Government was led by V.I. Lenin. He was the leader of the Bolshevik party.

In October 1917, the Bolsheviks organized an armed uprising ( October Revolution) . The people demanded the transfer of land to peasants, factories–to workers; they also demanded the end of the war. The Provisional Government was overthrown. It no longer governed the country. The Bolsheviks came to power. They created the Bolshevik government - the Council of People's Commissars (Sovnarkom) . It was V.I. Lenin (1917-1924) who became the head of the new government. In Russia, there was established the power of the Soviets of Workers' and Soldiers' Deputies (Soviet power).

As a result of the revolution, important socio-economic changes took place in the country. Private ownership, including that of land, was abolished. All plants, factories and banks became state property (they were nationalized). An eight-hour working day was introduced;child labor was prohibited; equal rights of women and men were proclaimed.

In 1918, the church was separated from the state, and the school was separated from the church.

The Bolsheviks proclaimed the equality of all nations and nationalities and the right of nations to self-determination. In December 1917, Poland and Finland gained the right to independence. In January 1918, there was created the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic (RSFSR). In March 1918, Soviet Russia signed a peace agreement with Germany and came out of the First World War, which for other countries ended in November 1918.

However, after the Bolsheviks came to power, in Russia there broke out the Civil War. At the same time, other foreign states began to intervene in the internal life of Russia. The main military force in the fight against the Bolsheviks and Soviet power was the White Movement (White Army). The White Army consisted of former officers of the tsarist army and members of various political parties. It fought against the Red Army, which had been created by the Bolsheviks.

In 1921, the Civil War ended with the victory of the Bolsheviks. Soviet power was established in Central Asia and the Caucasus.
