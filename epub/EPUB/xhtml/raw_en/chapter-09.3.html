9.3

Perestroika in the USSR (1985-1991)

Perestroika in the USSR (1985-1991)

М.С. Горбачёв © Владимир Вяткин, Архив РИА Новости

In 1985, M.S. Gorbachev became the leader of the USSR (1985-1991). There began economic and political reforms in the country. That period was called perestroika (restructuring).

In the economy, the task was set to accelerate the pace of economic development, primarily in engineering, and to improve discipline. There began the fight against alcoholism. Product quality control was enhanced. There was developing individual (private) labor activity of citizens in various fields.

However, from 1990 the economic situation became worse: the country lacked food and industrial goods.

At the same time, in the USSR there was being implemented a reform of the political system. A multi-party system began to take shape. In addition to the CPSU, new parties were formed - social-democratic, liberal, national-patriotic ones.

The system of the USSR government changed. The Congress of People's Deputies began to work again. It was the highest legislative body of the country. The Congress formed the Supreme Soviet of the USSR (in fact, parliament).

In March 1990, M.S. Gorbachev became the first President of the USSR. In1991, there were held presidential elections in the union republics.

During the years of perestroika, important changes took place in the social life of the USSR. There was more freedom; information became available. People openly expressed their opinions at rallies, referendums, and elections. It contributed to the development of the spiritual life of Soviet people.

It was literature that played a special role. Writers and poets wrote about the problems that had not been discussed previously. Among them were the results of the Great October Socialist Revolution, the role of Lenin and Stalin in the history of the country, the repressions, etc.

There were changes in the position of religion in the USSR. Christian culture and culture of other religions were developing freely; temples were returned to believers. Representatives of the clergy could openly express their opinions in the Soviet press, on radio, television, and speak about the problems of education.

However, at the end of perestroika a crisis began in the country. The opponents of Gorbachev (including President of the RSFSR B.N. Yeltsin) demanded more radical reforms.

During perestroika, changes took place in the foreign policy of the USSR. The relations between the East and the West improved. In February 1989, the Soviet Union completed the withdrawal of its troops from Afghanistan. After that there were normalized the relations with China.

At the same time, there began the crisis of the socialist system. In Central and Eastern Europe, the communist regimes were abolished. Thus, the USSR lost its allies in the socialist camp. The international position of the Soviet Union weakened.
