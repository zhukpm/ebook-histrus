Period of radical change in the Great Patriotic War

In the period from the autumn of 1942 to the end of 1943,there occurred the radical change in the course of the war. In the summer of 1942, the German troops came to the North Caucasus and the Volga River. There began the defense of Stalingrad (today it is the city of Volgograd)..

In November, the Battle of Stalingrad began –it was the decisive battle of the Great Patriotic War. It ended with the victory of the Red Army. A large group of German troops, including 24 generals, was captured. As a result of the general offensive of the Red Army in January 1943, the siege of Leningrad was broken. By the summer, a significant part of the North Caucasus had been liberated.

The population helped the Soviet troops under the slogan "Everything for the front, everything for the victory!" The main food bases during the war years were the districts of the Volga region, Siberia, Kazakhstan, and Central Asia. The Russian Orthodox Church and representatives of other religious denominations strengthened the spirit of the Red Army and provided it with material assistance.

In the occupied territory, Nazi Germany resorted to terror. There were created concentration camps. Nazi Germany pursued the policy of slaughter of the Slavic and Jewish population. In total, in the occupied territories about seven million civilians and about four million prisoners of war died. More than five million people were taken away for work to Germany.

In the summer of 1943 , the Battle of Kursk took place. The Soviet troops liberated the cities of Oryol, Belgorod, Kharkov. In November, the capital of Ukraine, the city of Kiev, was liberated.
