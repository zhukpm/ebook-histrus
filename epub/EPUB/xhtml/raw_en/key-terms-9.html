Cult of personality - exaltation of an individual personality - of a statesman.

Nobel Prizes - international prizes named after their founder, Swedish chemical engineer Alfred Nobel. The Nobel Prizes have been awarded annually (since 1901) for outstanding work in the fields of physics, chemistry, medicine and physiology, economics, as well as for literary works and for activities to strengthen peace.

Perestroika - general name for the reforms and new ideology of the USSR party leadership, which were carried out in 1985-1991 on the initiative of M.S. Gorbachev, the General Secretary of the CPSU Central Committee.

Satellite - unmanned vehicle that is not capable of independently flying into space. To this end, special aircraft are used (manned spacecraft).

The Cold War - international situation characterized by military-political confrontation between states, primarily the USSR and the USA and their allies. It lasted from 1945 to 1991 - until the collapse of the USSR and the fall of communist regimes in the countries of Central and Eastern Europe.

Virgin soil - new, poorly developed lands in Kazakhstan, the Volga region, Siberia, the Urals, the Far East. In the USSR, mass development of virgin soils was carried out in 1954-1960.
