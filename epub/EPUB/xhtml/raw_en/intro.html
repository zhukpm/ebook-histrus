Introduction

The textbook on history of Russia is aimed at helping people who come to Russia from different countries, speak different languages and have different cultures and religions, to obtain the knowledge necessary for an adequate understanding of their sociocultural environment, to overcome ethnic isolation and for equal communication with local people and each other.

The textbook highlights the most important milestones in the history of Russia from ancient times to the present day, which contributes to foreign nationals’ formation of a holistic understanding of the main stages of the historical development of Russia’s peoples, the peculiarities of the relationship between the Russians and the peoples that were part of the Russian Empire and the USSR, as well as with the peoples of modern Russia.

The material presented in the textbook introduces spiritual and material life, traditions and culture, labor and military exploits of the peoples of Russia, which ultimately aims to form an objective idea of Russia's contribution to the development of world history and culture.

Studying the course of history will help foreign nationals to better navigate the new conditions, including the sociocultural sphere of communication.

The textbook is designed for both classroom training and independent work.

The textbook is built on chronological basis. It consists of four sections, including the main themes, which contain only the most important historic facts, events and concepts. It is recommended to study them in the process of introductory classes or independently.

Studying history of Russia provides an opportunity for foreign nationals to get acquainted with the main events of the past, traditions, cultural and labor achievements of the peoples of Russia. In the textbook, the formation and development of Russian society is presented as a complex historical process that took place on a multinational and multi-religious basis, in which there prevailed tolerance and toleration, as well as interaction among peoples, which at different times were part of the Russian state.
