Socio-economic changes in the Russian Federation

In 2000, after the resignation of B.N. Yeltsin, V.V. Putin was elected the new president of Russia (2000-2004). He set the task to restore law and order in the country as well as to strengthen the unity of the Russian state.

In the economic policy, the government began the fight against poverty. In the country foreign direct investment in the economy increased, and its recovery began.

В.В. Путин © putin.kremlin.ru

For the first time in 50 years, Russia began to sell its grain abroad. There was paid off the external debt to Western creditors. The state paid pensions, salaries, and child allowances in a timely manner. However, a quarter of the population still had an income below the minimum subsistence level.

In the country there were carried out the following reforms: judicial, military, housing and communal services; reforms of education, medicine, pension provision. In the republics of the North Caucasus, and above all in the Chechen Republic, the work on building peaceful life continued.

During V.V. Putin’s second presidential term (2004-2008), there began the implementation of the national projects led by Prime Minister D.A. Medvedev. They were aimed at ensuring that people have good housing, education and medical care.

There was adopted the state program for the development of new technologies in the defense industry, agriculture, medicine and space.

In 2008-2012,the president of Russia was D.A. Medvedev, the Prime Minister was V.V. Putin. During that period, the government implemented a set of measures to overcome the 2008 financial crisis. One of the important tasks for the state was the fight against corruption.

In March 2012, V.V. Putin was elected President of Russia again; the Chairman of the government was D.A. Medvedev. The country's leadership set the task of creating a rich and prosperous Russia.

In January2020, M.V. Mishustin became Chairman of the Government of the Russian Federation. The task was set to improve the living standards in the country, to solve the demographic problems, to support families with children and provide social assistance to the population. There was continued the work on the national projects. One of them is the national project "Digital Economy". Its goal is to stimulate the technological development of the economy, as well as to digitalize the real sector.

М.В. Мишустин © Пресс-служба Госдумы

The country adopted state programs to increase childbirth and social assistance to families with children. As a result, for the first time the birth rate in Russia increased.

It is the migration policy of Russia that plays an important role in the modern conditions. Modern migration provides various sectors of the Russian economy with workers and other specialists. At present, there are more than 2 million legal migrants in Russia. The state is taking measures for foreign citizens’ adaptation and integration into Russian society.

On January 1, 2015, there was introduced an examination in the Russian language, history of Russia and the fundamentals of the legislation of the Russian Federation for foreign citizens who apply for permission to work, obtaining a patent, temporary residence and a residence permit in the Russian Federation. The purpose of the exam is to create conditions for the socio-cultural, language and legal adaptation of foreign citizens in Russia.
