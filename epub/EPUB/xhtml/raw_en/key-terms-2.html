Basic facts (lesson 2)

Basic concepts

The Boyar Duma - high council at the Grand Duke (from 1547 - at the Tsar).It consisted of the representatives of aristocracy; its activities were deliberative.

Nobility - the term "noblemen" has been known since the end of the XII century. Before the beginning of the XIV century, it had denoted people who were in the military service of the prince. From the XIV century, noblemen were given land (mainly an estate as property for the period of service).

The Zemsky Sobor - central nationwide estate-representative institutions with law-advisory functions in the middle of the ХVI-ХVII centuries. The first sobor was convened in 1549. At the Zemsky Sobors, there were discussed and resolved the most important issues of internal and foreign policy of Russia; at the Zemsky Sobors tsars were elected (Boris Godunov – in 1598, Vasily Shuisky - in 1606, Mikhail Fyodorovich Romanov -in 1613).

Estate - social group of a traditional society that possesses rights and obligations, which are enshrined in custom or law and inherited.

Sudebnik of 1497 - collection of laws that played an important role in the unification of the Russian state and the creation of all-Russian law.

Tsar (from Latin caesar ) - in Russia it was the official title of the head of state in 1547-1721.
