Cultural and spiritual life of Russian society

In modern Russia, important changes are taking place in the cultural and spiritual life.

In recent years, there has begun uplift in science and education. Such scientific areas as microelectronics, computer science and biotechnology are actively developing. For their further development, the Skolkovo Innovation Center was created in Moscow. Recently, special attention has been paid to the study of man and his health, the solution to environmental problems.

There are significant achievements in the field of physics. Russian scientists were awarded the Nobel Prize - Zh.I. Alferov in 2000, V.L. Ginzburg and A.A. Abrikosov - in 2003.

The reform of education is being conducted in the country, and its quality is improving. Higher education institutions are switched to multilevel training of students according to the Western model (bachelor - master - doctor of philosophy (PhD)). The Academy of Sciences of the Russian Federation and its branch institutes have also been reformed.

In modern Russia, theater, cinema, painting, architecture are actively developing. There are well-known theater directors and film directors - P.S. Lungin, N.S. Mikhalkov (the Chairman of the Russian Cinematographers' Union) and others.

It is classical music that holds the important place in the life of Russian people. M.L. Rostropovich was a world-famous musician. Currently, widely known musicians are V.A. Gergiev, Yu.A. Bashmet, D.L. Matsuev that by their talent multiply the glory of Russia.

In the contemporary Russian art there are various trends and genres. I.S. Glazunov is the famous contemporary artist, the founder of the Russian Academy of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture; the author of numerous works on the history of Russia and Russian Orthodox culture ("Rus", "Mystery of the XX century", "Kulikovo Field", "Eternal Russia", etc.).

Famous muralist and sculptor, President of the Russian Academy of Arts Z.K. Tsereteli contributed greatly to promoting Russian art. One of his famous works is the monument to Peter I in Moscow.

In the spiritual sphere, the state sets the task of preserving and strengthening the moral traditions of society, the ideas of patriotism, respect and love of the motherland and its history. In 2012,there was created the Russian Historical Society which does a lot to disseminate historical knowledge, particularly among young people.

In Russia, there are new public holidays associated with important historic events. June 12 is Russia Day (the day of the adoption of the Declaration of State Sovereignty); November 4 is the National Unity Day(it is associated with the historic date - the victory of the militia led by K. Minin and D. Pozharsky over the Polish intervention in 1612 and the end of the Time of Troubles in Russia); December 12 is the Constitution Day of the Russian Federation. A special place in the life of Russian people is occupied by the Victory Day, which is celebrated on May 9 and associated with the victory of the Soviet people in the Great Patriotic War.

In addition, in Russia there are such holidays as New Year (January 1), Defender of the Fatherland Day (February 23), International Women's Day (March 8).

The state is actively engaged in the promotion of sports and a healthy lifestyle. In 2014, the XXII Winter Olympic Games were successfully held in Sochi. The Russian Olympic team took first place in the total number of medals.

In 2018,for the first time in its history, Russia became the host country of the Football World Cup (it was first held in Eastern Europe). The FIFA Council recognized this championship as the best in history.

The traditional religions of the peoples of Russia play an important role in their lives. The main religions are Christianity - mainly Orthodoxy (up to 70%), Islam (7%), as well as Buddhism and Judaism.

It is the Russian Orthodox Church that plays the unifying role in the life of the country. In 2009,at the Church Council Metropolitan Kirill was elected Patriarch of Moscow and All Russia. The residence of the Patriarch is located in the Danilov Monastery in Moscow.

Orthodox people have many religious holidays. Of these, the most important and revered are Christmas (January 7), Epiphany (January 19), Easter. The holiday of Epiphany is especially significant in Russia. On the night of Epiphany, it is customary to consecrate water in the temple, which after that is considered healthy. People prayerfully plunge into cold water. 

Easter is another important Orthodox holiday - the Holy Resurrection of the Lord Jesus Christ. On this day, people have festive food on the tables - Easter cakes, eggs painted red. Orthodox believers greet each other with exclamations: “Christ is Risen!” - “Truly He is Risen!” –and kiss three times.

The Muslim community of Russia actively participates in the life of the country, makes a significant contribution to the development of inter-faith dialogue, fruitfully interacts with state and public organizations in the affairs of charity and education of the younger generation. It promotes the preservation of the rich cultural and spiritual heritage of the peoples of Russia.

One of the important centralized religious organizations of Muslims of Russia is the Council of Muftis of Russia. The Chairman of the Council is Mufti Sheikh Ravil Gainutdin. In the capital of Russia,there is located the Moscow Cathedral Mosque (built in 1904). It houses the Council of Muftis of Russia and the residence of its Chairman. The Muslims of Russia celebrate all their most important holidays - Nowruz, Ramadan, Uraza-bairam and Kurban-bairam, etc.

Orthodox Churches, mosques, Buddhist temples, synagogues are special places.

Each religion provides its own norms and rules of conduct in the house of God. The general norms of behavior in all churches are as follows: do not smoke, do not drink alcohol, do not make noise, behave with respect to the place of presence of God. All other rules are very different.

When entering the Orthodox Church, a man is to remove his headdress. Women should not enter the holy temple in trousers and short skirts. A woman's head should be covered. When visiting temples, one is supposed to dress so that most of the body is covered. It is not customary to go to the temple in shorts and sportswear. During the service in the Orthodox Church, people pray while standing, only sick and elderly people can sit.

Mosque is a house of prayer for Muslims. The floor of a mosque is carpeted; shoes are left at the entrance. There can be a common entrance or separate entrances to the mosque for men and women. In a mosque, women usually pray on the second floor or in the fenced part of the first floor. The appearance of a visitor to a mosque should not offend believers: one should wear clean, modest and adequately closed clothing. Women should have the entire body covered, with the exception of face, hands and feet. Women should completely hide their hair under a scarf..

In Russia, there are representatives of different religions. All of them coexist peacefully in one country; they respect the customs and traditions of each other. This allows the peoples of Russia to maintain inter-faith harmony and to be open for a dialogue with foreign citizens who come to Russia from different countries of the world.
