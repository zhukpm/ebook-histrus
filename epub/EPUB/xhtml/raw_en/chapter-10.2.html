Socio-political processes in the Russian Federation

Конституция Российской Федерации © kremlin.ru

In new Russia there began the formation of the rule-of-law state. The political rights and freedoms of people expanded.

B.N. Yeltsin was a supporter of market reforms. The Supreme Soviet of the RSFSR led by R.I. Khasbulatov opposed such reforms. There began political struggle between them. In the autumn of 1993,a constitutional and political crisis occurred in the country. It resulted in the victory of President B.N. Yeltsin. At his suggestion, on December 12, 1993, at a popular referendum there was adopted the new Constitution. The Constitution is the main law of the state. December 12 is a national holiday in Russia–Constitution Day.

Under the new Constitution, a new name for the country appeared - the Russian Federation (RF). The Federal Assembly (parliament) became the new legislative body. It consists of two houses: the Upper House is the Federation Council, the Lower House, the State Duma, is an elected body. Elections to the State Duma were held every four years.

The head of the executive branch is the President of the Russian Federation. The residence of the President is located in the Moscow Kremlin.

In Russia, there also appeared new state symbols: the coat of arms, the flag and the anthem of the Russian Federation.

Under the Constitution, Russia is a democratic federal state with the republican form of government. Russia consists of different constituent entities of the Federation. In 1993, the country had 89 constituent entities of the Federation (currently there are 85 of them). The constituent entities of the Federation are autonomous republics, regions, territories and three federal cities - Moscow, St. Petersburg and Sevastopol (since 2014).

In the 1990s, some republics wanted to gain complete independence and secede from Russia. Therefore, President Yeltsin faced the task of strengthening the unity of the state. In 1992, Moscow signed the Federal Agreement with the constituent entities of the Federation. It determined the boundaries and rights of the constituent entities of the Federation as part of a single state.

As a result of the reforms of the 1990s, great changes took place in the social and cultural life of Russian people. Life became more diverse. There appeared private art galleries, television companies, publishing houses, etc. There began to actively develop international scientific and cultural ties, student exchanges.

At the same time, due to financial difficulties, many scientists and cultural workers left the country. It led to dire consequences in the country.
