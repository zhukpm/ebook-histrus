Collapse of the USSR and its consequences

Collapse of the USSR and its consequences

The USSR was a multinational federation. At the end of the 1980s, it included 15 union republics. During the years of Soviet power, in the USSR there were created conditions for the development of nations and national cultures.

Towards the end of perestroika, the economic and political situation in the USSR aggravated. There began the crisis of the communist ideology and the ruling party - the CPSU.

The rise of national movements began in different republics - in Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Armenia, Georgia, and Ukraine. The republics, including Russia, adopted declarations of state sovereignty.

In those conditions, M.S. Gorbachev wanted to reform the USSR. On March 17, 1991, a referendum was held in the USSR. The majority of the USSR citizens (76.4%) voted in favor of preserving the Soviet Union. However, a new treaty between the republics was not signed, since in August 1991 the opponents of perestroika tried to stage a coup and oust Gorbachev.

It accelerated the collapse of the USSR. It was the Baltic republics that were the first to secede from the country. Then other union republics declared their independence.

On December 8, 1991, the leaders of Russia, Ukraine and Byelorussia signed the Agreement on the Dissolution of the Soviet Union.

At the same time, there was signed the Agreement on the establishment of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS).

After the collapse of the USSR, the political, economic and cultural ties between the republics worsened, interethnic relations aggravated, for example, between Armenia and Azerbaijan, Georgia and South Ossetia, and others. There arose a problem of refugees.

Another problem was the situation of the Russian-speaking population (almost 25 million people) in the national republics. Thus, beyond the borders of Russia there was formed the Russian world. It consists of people who do not live in Russia, but speak Russian, respect Russian culture, history and maintain ties with Russia.
