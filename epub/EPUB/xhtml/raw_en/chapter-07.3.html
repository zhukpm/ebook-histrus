Socio-economic and political development of the USSR in the 1930s

Socio-economic and political development of the USSR in the 1930s

И.В. Сталин

After the death of V.I. Lenin, Joseph Stalin (1924-1953) became the head of the USSR. In the country, there was restored the economy destroyed during the Civil War. There was set the task of industrialization of the USSR, i.e. the creation of large-scale machine production in industry.

In the country there began the construction of plants, factories, power plants. The military industry was created. Thus, there were laid the foundations for the victory of the USSR in the Great Patriotic War.

At the same time, changes were taking place in agriculture. In the 1930s, in villages there was carried out collectivization, i.e. the creation of large collective farms (kolkhozes). In collective farms, people used machinery (cars, tractors). However, not all peasants wanted to join collective farms; some of them wanted to work independently. The government applied repression against peasants and forced many of them to settle in Siberia. The situation in villages was difficult: grain production decreased; there began famine. Only in the second half of the 1930s, the situation in agriculture began to improve.

The results of the industrialization of the USSR were quite contradictory. Over the years, about 9,000 new plants and factories were built in the country. There emerged new industries: automobile, tractor, chemical, aircraft, and others. In terms of industrial production, the Soviet Union ranked second in the world after the USA.

Industrialization changed the economies of all the national republics. For example, in Central Asia there were built new canals, power plants, as well as the Turkestan-Siberian Railway (Turksib), which connected Siberia with Central Asia and Kazakhstan.

Important changes took place in the life of Soviet society. There was no longer unemployment in the USSR. People gained the right to free education and medical care. Workers gained the right to annual leave and pensions, working women - the right to maternity leave. However, the living standards of Soviet people were lower than in Western Europe and the USA.

At that time, in the USSR there was a rigid political system of power. There was one party in the country - the Bolshevik Party (from 1952 - the Communist Party of the Soviet Union (CPSU)). At the same time, in the country there was established the regime of personal power (or personality cult) of the party’s General Secretary — Joseph Stalin. The party established strict control over the state and carried out repressions against various sectors of society. Many people died. However, it was impossible to establish complete control of power over society.
